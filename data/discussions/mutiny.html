Architecture of Mutiny

Mutiny is built on Godot 4.4 using C# and features a modular architecture that enables easy expansion of game mechanics and content. The core gameplay revolves around a deck-building system where players acquire and upgrade cards to face increasingly challenging enemies.

Data-Driven Card System

Cards are designed as data-driven entities, with their behavior implemented through scriptable objects (Resource in Godot) and a flexible event system. This approach enables rapid iteration on game design while maintaining a clean and maintainable codebase : new cards and mechanics can be added without major code changes.

Example: The Blunderbuss Card

The data-driven architecture allows complex card behaviors to be defined declaratively. When the Blunderbuss card is activated:

  • Deals 10 damage (DamageEffect)
  • Applies effects to the next cards in deck (AffectOtherCardsEffect):
    • Sets them inactive (InactiveEffect)

Card descriptions are automatically generated from their defined effects, ensuring consistency across the entire card pool while enabling rapid creation of unique card combinations and strategic interactions.

Rogue-lite Progression

Mutiny features a rogue-lite progression system where players begin each run with a basic deck and build their arsenal through strategic choices. Each run presents encounters with escalating difficulty, requiring careful deck composition and tactical decision-making.

Card Pool and Rarity System

Cards are organized into five rarity tiers (1 to 5) that determine their power level and availability. As players progress, they unlock access to higher rarity cards offering greater strategic depth and powerful synergies. Card selection is governed by weighted randomness based on rarity distribution, creating unpredictable scenarios that enhance replayability and encourage adaptive strategies.

Statistics and the Bribe Mechanic

Players acquire permanent statistics that fundamentally alter their playstyle. The Bribe mechanic allows spending in-game currency to boost these stats, adding economic strategy to the gameplay loop. Each of the four main stats influences card drops and reward probabilities, creating personalized runs tailored to different playstyles and deck archetypes.
